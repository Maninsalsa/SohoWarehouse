Picante de la Casa

Build:
chili pepper, cilantro in tin
.75 agave
3oz batch (1oz lime, 2oz repo tequila)

Method:
All ingredients in tin. Hard shake, double strain

Glass: Rocks Glass

Garnish: Red Hat

The picante de la casa combines some of the ingredients of the margarita with chili and coriander, which add a little extra heat and spice. It is based on the much-loved Tommy's margarita, built up with ingredients that are popular at our HOuses in Miami, New York and London.

It's a spicy marg with repo tequila.

Cocktail Name Cocktail Build Taste Notes

Soho Nacional

Specs:
.50oz Lime juice
1oz Pineapple juice
.25oz Simple
2oz Batch (.50oz Bacardi Ocho, 1oz Bacardi Superior, .50oz St-Germain)

Glass: Coupe

Garnish: Dehydrated Lime

Mods: No

Batched: Yes

This cocktail is a riff on the classic Hotel Nacional, with the floral, lychee flavor of St-Germain replacing the apricot liqueur. White rum is split with a little bit of aged rum to give a bit of a caramelized flavor to the pineapple juice.

The Bridgetown

Specs:
3 Dash fea foam
2 Dash cranberry bitters
1 Dash ango bitters
1oz Lime juice
1oz Soursop syrup
2oz Ron Zacapa

Glass: Nick n Nora

Garnish: Cran bitter heart

Mods: YES

An indulgent blend of Ron Zacapa's aged richness and the tropical allure of soursop syrup, brightened with zesty lime. Cranberry and Angostura bitters add a subtle edge, while Fea Foam smooths it all into a silky, elegant finish. A cocktail that's both adventurous and refined, like a stroll through a rainforest at golden hour.

Paloma

Specs:
Pinch of salt
.50oz Lime juice
2oz 400-conejos mezcal
Top off with Grapefruit soda

Glass: Collins

Garnish: Grapefruit wedge

Mods: YES

The paloma – the Spanish word for 'dove' – is by far the most popular way of drinking tequila in Mexico (other than swigging it straight from the bottle). There are many different brands of grapefruit soda that can be used in this drink, including Jarritos, Squirt and Ting.

Strawberry Fields

Specs:
.75oz Lemon
.75oz Simple syrup
3oz batch (.50oz Apricot Liquor, .50oz St-Germain, 1.5oz Tanq 10 -strawberry infused-)
Top off with club soda

Glass: Collins

Garnish: None

Mods: NO

Batched: Yes

An old DTLA classic makes it return this summer. A vibrant, effervescent blend of strawberry-infused gin, delicately sweetened with St-Germain and apricot liqueur. Fresh lemon juice adds a crisp citrus tang, balanced by a touch of simple syrup, and finished with a splash of club soda for a light, sparkling finish. Floral, fruity, and refreshingly!

Aperol Spritz

Specs:
2oz Aperol
3oz Prosecco
top off Club soda

Glass: Wine

Garnish: Orange wedge

Mods: NO

A classic Italian aperitif cocktail made popular in the northern part of Italy, the Aperol spritz dates back to the end of the 19th century, when Venice was still part of the Austrian empire. The cocktail is based on the (then) popular Austrian spritz, which was a combination of white wine and soda water.

Boulevardier

Specs:
1oz Sweet Vermouth
1oz Campari
1oz Bulleit bourbon

Glass: Coupe

Garnish: Orange zest, peel in

Mods: YES

The boulevardier was first invented during Prohibition and was known as the signature drink of writer Erskine Gwynne. Gwynne left America for Paris in the 1920s and became the editor of a monthly publication called — what else? — Boulevardier.

NA Beverages

Beverage Name Build Taste Notes
Pick Me Up

Build:
1.5oz espresso
.75oz Three Spirit Livener
1.5oz Seedlip Spice

Method:
Combine all ingredients in a shaker and add ice. Short shake and strain into a Nick & Nora.

Glass: Nick & Nora

Garnish: One espresso bean

The Espresso Martini is one of the best-sold cocktails at Soho Houses across NAM. This version keeps the simplicity of the original, with a little heat and fresh citrus to brighten the finish.

SFW

Build:
.5oz pineapple-jalapeño syrup
1oz lime juice
1.5oz Pentire Coastal Spritz

Method:
Combine all ingredients in a shaker and add ice. Shake, then strain and double strain into an rocks glass.

Glass: Rocks

Garnish: Dehydrated lime wheel, mint

The NA cocktail is a blend about vibrant citrus (at least a holiday with sun) and a kick of heat. Dehydrated and citrused before, while the earthy mint harmonizes in the close.

Vibrante Spritz

Build:
1oz Martini Vibrante
3oz TT Tonic

Method:
Combine all ingredients in a wine glass with ice and stir to blend.

Glass: Wine

Garnish: Orange

This cocktail swaps out traditional alcohol for a vibrant, botanical base, gently sweetened, perfectly complemented by aromatic and citrused tonic. Super fresh!

Palomar

Build:
.5oz Pentire Adrift
.75oz Three Spirit Livener
Top with FT grapefruit soda
Pinch of salt

Method:
Combine all ingredients (except soda) into a highball glass. Add ice then top with soda and finish with a very small pinch of salt.

Glass: Highball

Garnish: None

This cocktail is a riff on a Paloma, but with the added complexity of Three Spirits Livener, which brings schisandra berry, guava, green tea, and other energizing botanicals to the mix. Please note: Three Spirits Livener is very high in caffeine.

Eastern Standard Fizz

Build:
3 slices cucumber, 3 mint leaves
.5oz lime juice
.5oz simple syrup
1.5oz Seedlip Garden
Top with soda water

Method:
Add all ingredients to tin except the soda and muddle the cucumber and mint. Shake with ice and double strain into a highball. Top with soda water.

Glass: Highball

Garnish: Cucumber

The garden effect is fresh, clean, and cooling. The "brunchy" herbal cocktail called the Eastern Standard is made in NAM. Also see "fancy brunch" China.
